# Representative user (persona)

> This file is reference context for the **T4-rich** brief. The app is a *generic* compound-centric strength app — not built for one named person. This persona describes the **target user archetype**; the individual's specifics (experience, schedule, lifts, starting numbers) are captured in **onboarding**, not hardcoded.

## Who the app is for

Compound-focused lifters who want to run a structured barbell program without overthinking it. They center training on the main barbell lifts — **squat, bench press, deadlift, overhead press** — plus whatever assistance their program prescribes.

- **Experience ranges from beginner to early-advanced.** Some know their working weights and 1RMs; some are guessing and want sensible starting points.
- **They train 3–6 days/week**, depending on schedule and the program they pick. (A new lifter on a 3-day full-body plan; a more advanced lifter on a 6-day split — both are in scope.)
- **They want the app to do the thinking:** what to train today, what weight to use, how to load the bar, and when to add weight. They don't want to do math mid-workout.
- **Real use is mid-workout:** sweaty hands, brief glances between sets, sometimes a bright gym, sometimes a dark one. Logging can't break their rest cadence.
- **They may switch programs as they progress** (e.g., off a linear beginner program onto 5/3/1) and expect to keep all their logged history.
- **They are not coaches.** If the app can recommend a sensible program from a few answers, they'll take the help — but they also want to be able to choose for themselves.

## What "good" feels like for them

- Open the app, see exactly what to do today, and start lifting in seconds.
- Never calculate a working weight or a plate load by hand.
- Trust that the program is progressing them correctly and that their history is safe.
- Feel a small sense of progress (a PR, a trend line moving up) without digging for it.
