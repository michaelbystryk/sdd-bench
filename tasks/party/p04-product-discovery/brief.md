# Help us figure out why new accounts stall

I run product at Stacklet, a no-code internal-tools builder. Signups are growing
but a lot of new accounts never get to a tool their team actually uses — and the
people around me each have a different theory about why, none of which I fully
believe yet. I've written up where things stand, the numbers I trust and the ones
I don't, and the conflicting stories I'm hearing, in `reference/problem.md`.

I do **not** want a feature list, and I don't want to just back whichever team
argued hardest. Before we spend a quarter building "guided onboarding" or an
"editor redesign" or "more power features," I want to understand the problem
properly and then find out the truth cheaply.

What would genuinely help me:

- A clear framing of what the problem actually is — sharper than how I've stated
  it, and honest about what we do and don't know.
- Your best theories for why new accounts stall, ordered by how likely you think
  each is given the context, with your reasoning.
- For the direction you find most credible, the beliefs we'd be *betting on* that
  would hurt the most if they turned out to be wrong.
- A way to actually find out — cheap, fast checks I could run in the next week or
  two rather than a quarter-long build, and for each one, what result would tell
  me I'm right versus wrong.

Be willing to disagree with the loudest voices in the doc if the context points
elsewhere. I'd rather you tell me my framing is off than confirm it.

## Deliverable

Produce `discovery.md` as a standalone Markdown document with these sections:
**Problem framing**; **Hypotheses (ranked)**; **Riskiest assumptions**; **Discovery
plan** (the cheapest test per assumption, and what result would validate vs.
invalidate it). Target length: ~2 pages (roughly 800–1,200 words); a short
hypotheses or assumptions table is fine and doesn't count against length.

## A note on ambiguity

If anything is ambiguous, make a reasonable assumption and tag it [ASSUMPTION].
