# Lumen — company & product context

*Internal one-pager for whoever's picking up the H2 strategy work. Background only;
the raw signal is in the other files in this folder.*

## What we sell

Lumen is a B2B SaaS accounts-payable (AP) tool. A finance team forwards or uploads
vendor invoices; Lumen does the OCR/data-extraction, routes each invoice for approval
to the right people, and stores an approved, coded record. We sit between the inbox
and whatever the customer pays out of. Think "the layer that turns a pile of PDF
invoices into clean, approved, ready-to-pay records."

We do **not** move money (no payments rail) and we do **not** replace the ERP /
accounting system. Customers still post the approved invoices into NetSuite, Sage
Intacct, QuickBooks, Xero, etc. — today mostly by hand or CSV.

## Who buys

- **Core:** mid-market finance teams, roughly 50–500 employees. Buyer is the
  Controller or AP Manager. ~140 paying accounts here. This is ~80% of ARR.
- **Self-serve / small business:** under ~50 employees, mostly on the $49/mo
  free-to-cheap self-serve plan. ~900 accounts but tiny revenue; many are dormant.
- **Enterprise (new, unproven):** a handful of 1,000+ employee prospects sales has
  been chasing this year. Zero closed so far.

## Where we are

Founded 4 years ago. Got to ~$6.2M ARR on the core mid-market motion. Growth has
flattened over the last three quarters — new logos still come in but net revenue
retention slipped from ~108% to ~97%, i.e. we're now leaking through the bucket about
as fast as we fill it. Board wants a point of view on H2 and FY-next: where do we
double down, and what do we stop doing. We have engineering capacity for roughly
**two** meaningful bets in the next two quarters, not five.

The pricing is per-seat + an invoice-volume tier. Gross margin is healthy (~78%).
