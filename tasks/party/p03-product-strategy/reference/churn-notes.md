# Churn & downgrade notes — CS team, last two quarters

Free-form notes the CS team logs in the account record when someone downgrades, leaves,
or signals they're at risk. Verbatim-ish, lightly cleaned. ARR figures are annual.

---

**Northwind Freight** — $42k ARR — CHURNED
Left for [Competitor A]. Reason in the exit call: "Your routing is better but they sync
to NetSuite natively and you don't. We were spending ~6 hours a week re-keying. The math
stopped working." Stayed 2.5 years. Would have renewed if reconciliation were solved.

**Castle & Pike LLP** — $28k ARR — CHURNED
Law firm. Left for an all-in-one practice-management suite that bundles AP. Honestly not
our buyer — they wanted matter-level cost allocation we never claimed to do. Low-fit from
the start. Not really winnable.

**Bellweather SaaS** — $51k ARR — DOWNGRADED (from 40 seats to 18)
Didn't leave but cut seats hard. Note: "They did a finance-team reorg and most approvers
moved to Slack-only. The people who actually log in dropped." Routing + Slack approvals
are why they stayed at all. At-risk if a competitor matches Slack approvals.

**Greenfield Components** — $36k ARR — CHURNED
"The CSV-to-Sage dance broke twice during a month-end close and we missed our close
deadline. We can't have a tool that adds a manual step to the most important day of our
month." Reconciliation again. Was a promoter on routing in Q1, churned in Q3.

**Halcoyon Media** — $19k ARR — CHURNED
Small (35 ppl). Bought on the self-serve plan, upgraded, then left. "Got too expensive
for what we use it for — we only really use the OCR." Price-sensitive, light user. The
kind of account that probably shouldn't have been on a mid-market plan.

**Tindall Manufacturing** — $44k ARR — AT RISK (renews in 90 days)
"Renewal call: controller said the re-keying into Intacct is the #1 internal complaint.
Asked point-blank if reconciliation is on the roadmap. I had nothing to tell her." CS
flagged this as winnable-if-we-act.

**Orsino Health** — $63k ARR — DOWNGRADED then re-expanded
Briefly cut back during a freeze, came back when we shipped conditional approval limits
in beta to them. Data point: routing depth drove an expansion.

---

**CS lead summary note:** "Of the five real churns above, three name reconciliation /
ERP re-keying as the deciding factor (Northwind, Greenfield, and Tindall is heading
the same way). One was never our fit (Castle & Pike). One was a too-small price-sensitive
account (Halcyon). The accounts we lose for *product* reasons, we lose on the back end —
the handoff into their accounting system — not the front end. We almost never lose on
OCR quality or routing; routing is usually why they stayed as long as they did."
