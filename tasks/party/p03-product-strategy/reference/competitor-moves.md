# Competitor moves — what we've seen in the last ~2 quarters

Compiled by product marketing from release notes, LinkedIn, sales-call intel, and a
couple of lost-deal debriefs. Confidence varies; noted where it's soft.

---

## Competitor A — "Synthesis AP" (our closest mid-market rival)

- **Native NetSuite + Sage Intacct sync, GA.** Two-way: approved invoices post straight
  into the ERP with GL coding carried over; reconciliation status flows back. This is
  the thing Northwind and the multi-entity group (T-2314) left us for. **High
  confidence** — we've seen it in two lost-deal debriefs.
- Their approval routing is, by multiple accounts, **weaker than ours** — single-level,
  no conditional rules. Customers who care about routing still prefer us.
- Pricing roughly at parity with ours.

> Read: Synthesis is winning on the back-end integration we lack, and losing on the
> front-end routing we lead. The two products are mirror images.

## Competitor B — "Ledgerly"

- Announced an **"AI Spend Copilot"** — a chat/agent layer that categorizes spend,
  flags anomalies, and answers questions like "how much did we spend on AWS last
  quarter?" Big launch, lots of LinkedIn buzz, a TechCrunch blurb. **Demo'd well.**
- BUT: Ledgerly sells primarily to **FP&A / finance-analytics** buyers, not AP
  operations. The Copilot is an *analytics* feature for a CFO-office persona — it sits
  on top of already-categorized spend data. It does not touch invoice intake, approval,
  or the AP workflow. Their AP capabilities are thin.
- We've had **two** mid-market prospects ask "do you have an AI copilot like Ledgerly's?"
  in demos this quarter. Sales is nervous about it.

> Read (product marketing's own caveat): the Copilot is impressive and loud, but it
> serves a different buyer and a different job-to-be-done than ours. Chasing it would
> put us in a feature race we'd enter late, against a different persona, away from where
> we win. Flagging because the buzz is loud relative to its actual relevance to our AP
> motion.

## Competitor C — legacy enterprise suite (Coupa-class)

- Plays up-market (2,000+ employees). Full procure-to-pay, vendor portals, SSO, the
  works. Slow, expensive, long implementations.
- Occasionally shows up in our enterprise prospect evals (the 1,400/2,000-person ones).
  We are not really competing in their weight class today and they're not chasing
  mid-market.

## General market note

The AP-automation category is consolidating around **"close the loop into the ERP."**
The narrative buyers increasingly expect is: invoice in → approved → *posted to the
accounting system* without re-keying. The OCR-and-route-only positioning we launched on
is becoming table stakes / the front half of a longer expected workflow.
