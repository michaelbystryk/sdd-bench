# H2 product strategy for Lumen

I run product at Lumen, a B2B SaaS for accounts-payable automation. We help mid-market
finance teams turn a pile of vendor invoices into clean, approved, ready-to-pay records
— OCR the invoice, route it for approval, store the coded result. (We don't move money
and we don't replace the customer's accounting system.) Full background is in
`reference/context.md`.

We're at an awkward point. We grew to ~$6.2M ARR on our core mid-market motion, but
growth has flattened for three quarters and net revenue retention has slipped below
100% — we're leaking about as fast as we fill. The board wants a clear point of view for
H2 and next fiscal year, and so do I. I'm tired of the strategy being "whatever was
loudest in last week's pipeline meeting."

The hard part is that the inputs pull in different directions and I don't fully trust all
of them. I've dumped the raw pile into `reference/`:

- `context.md` — company/product background and our constraints
- `support-tickets.md` — a slice of last quarter's support queue
- `churn-notes.md` — CS notes on accounts that left or downgraded
- `usage-stats.md` — product analytics from the last 90 days
- `competitor-moves.md` — what we've seen competitors ship
- `sales-asks.md` — the running "we'd close this deal if we had X" list

Some of it is contradictory, some of it is loud but probably doesn't matter, and some of
the metrics flatter us. I don't want a summary of these files back — I can read them. I
want you to figure out **what they actually add up to** and tell me where to point the
company.

The real constraint: we have engineering capacity for about **two** meaningful bets over
the next two quarters. Not five. So I need you to be willing to say no to things — and to
tell me what we should explicitly stop or decline, not just what to build. A list of
seven priorities is the same as no strategy.

What I'm looking for: a clear position on where we play and where we win, a small number
of real bets with the reasoning behind each, an explicit list of what we're NOT doing
(and why), and an order to do it all in. Be opinionated. If a popular internal belief is
wrong, say so and show me why from the signals.

## Deliverable

Produce `strategy.md` as a standalone Markdown document with these sections:
**Situation** (what the signals actually say — including which inputs you're
discounting and why); **Positioning** (where to play and how we win there); **Bets**
(3–5 bets, each with its rationale); **Kill-list** (what to stop, decline, or
de-prioritize, with reasoning); **Sequencing** (the order to execute, and what gates
each step). Target length: ~2–3 pages.
