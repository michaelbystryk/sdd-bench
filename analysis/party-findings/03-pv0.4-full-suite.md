## Findings

**1. Quality is a dead heat — the machinery adds nothing.** Across 11 tasks the four arms
land within **0.34 of a point** on average (A1 22.86, A2 22.98, A4 22.98, A3 23.20). **A4 —
the full multi-agent machinery at $1.35 — exactly ties A2 (extended thinking, $0.45) and sits
*below* A3 (a single persona prompt, $0.52).** Per-task, A4 ranges from best (P3, P9) to worst
(P2, P5); there is no task profile where the machinery reliably wins. The nominal top arm is
**A3 — one model prompted to roleplay a roundtable** — which is the masquerade itself.

**2. Cost is the only real difference: A4 ≈ 3×.** A4 averaged **$1.35** vs $0.44–0.52 for the
three solo-class arms (3.1× A1, 3.0× A2, 2.6× A3). On P5 it hit **$2.33 — 3.8×** — and lost on
quality. Every dollar of orchestration bought quality the raters scored as equal-or-worse.

**3. The machinery is invisible: ~94% of blind guesses were "A2."** Of 88 arm-guesses, raters
said "A2" (one model thinking long) ~83 times — across *every* true arm. **Party mode (A4) was
correctly identified on only 1 of 11 tasks (P4).** On P10 raters even fingered the *solo* arm
as A4. Persona-prompting (A3) and real orchestration (A4) leave no reliable signature in the
output a blind expert can detect.

**4. Recall saturated — and the only misses in the entire suite were A3/A4.** Every arm hit
the recall ceiling on every planted task except two cells: **P5 A3 & A4 (7/9)** and **P7 A3
(9/10)**. A1 and A2 never dropped below ceiling. So where recall moved at all, it moved
*against* the persona/party arms — on P5's derivation items (shared-work, capacity haircut)
the parallel-persona fan-out fragmented the cross-item synthesis a single reasoner (A2/A1)
completed. The machinery didn't just fail to help detection; on the hardest items it hurt.

**5. The precision-inflation prediction did NOT hold.** The masquerade hypothesis predicted
party mode's extra voices would generate *more* false positives. They didn't: FPs were
scattered and roughly even (A1 took 3 decoys on P5 + 1 on P1; A4 took ~2–3 on P5 + 1 on P10;
A2/A3 one each on P7). No arm was systematically noisier. Precision was a wash — recall and
cost carried the separation, not phantom findings.

**6. Party mode is operationally unreliable.** Headless, it **never self-landed the
deliverable** — every automated A4 (P2/P3/P7/P9/P10/P11) and one manual one (P5) stopped after
the roundtable to ask permission before writing the file; the deliverable only appeared after
a closing-line nudge. And it **leaks its own scaffolding** into the product: P8 ("the
roundtable (Amelia, Winston) preferred…") and P2 ("produced from a roundtable of independent
perspectives (Architect, Senior Engineer, Product…)") both required hand-scrubbing. It is also
**not a debate** — personas spawn as parallel one-shots with no cross-talk; the "roundtable"
fans out and concatenates.

## Verdict

The hypothesis ladder (PARTY-TRACK-BRIEF § Design) resolves to **A4 ≈ A3 ≈ A2 ≈ A1**: the
multi-agent machinery is **theater**. It is fully replicable by a single persona prompt (A3) at
a third of the cost — and that persona framing itself adds only ~0.3 pt over plain solo, well
inside rater noise. The quiet winner of the whole track is **A2, extended thinking**: it ties
the machinery on quality, leads on the one recall discriminator, costs the same as plain solo,
and is the thing party mode is supposed to beat. On a small reversible decision (P6) the
machinery is a measurable *tax*; on substantive advisory work it is a 3× cost premium for
output no one can distinguish from just asking the model to think.

## Caveats
- n=1 per cell; 2 raters; per-task quality deltas (~1–2 pts) are within noise — the findings
  rest on the *aggregate* (88-cell means, 88 arm-guesses) and the two clean directional
  signals (P5 recall split; A4's 3× cost).
- A4 mixed operator-mode (human P6/P1/P5/P8/P4; agent P2/P3/P7/P9/P10/P11). Default party mode,
  Opus personas throughout (Sonnet downgrade only on the trivial P6). Disclosed, not controlled.
- P1 & P7 recall came in above the keys' expected ceilings (10/10) — a salience re-check is
  warranted per those keys' own re-tune notes before a v-bump leans on them.
- Cross-model rater break (Opus cells / Fable raters) holds cleanly on the 7 non-security
  tasks; on P1/P8/P9/P10 Fable's classifier may have drifted raters toward Opus (recorded risk;
  objective recall/precision is near model-insensitive, so it mainly touches the rubric dims).
